Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 13, 2026

Eleven App Studios ("we," "our," or "us") operates Atlas AI (the "App"). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application built using the Unity game engine and published on the Google Play Store.

By installing and using the App, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

1. Information We Collect

A. Personal Data Provided Voluntarily

Because Atlas AI serves as an AI-powered study assistant, you provide information directly when interacting with our features:

  • Chat Logs and Prompts: We process the text, academic questions, and study prompts you enter into the user interface to generate educational insights and conversational responses.
  • User Account Information (Optional): If future updates allow you to create an optional cloud profile, we may securely collect your email address, name, or authentication credentials.

B. Data Collected Automatically by Third-Party SDKs

Our App is built using the Unity game engine and includes integrated tools to manage commerce and advertising. These third-party services automatically collect specific metrics for monetization, framework diagnostics, and fulfillment:

  • Google Mobile Ads SDK (AdMob): Collects device-specific data including your Internet Protocol (IP) address (for general location evaluation), unique advertising keys (such as the Android Advertising ID / AAID), product engagement statistics (ad views, clicks), and application telemetry.
  • Google Play Billing SDK (In-App Purchases): Manages secure premium upgrade transactions. We do not access, process, or store payment instruments or credit card combinations. Google Play securely archives purchase history indicators and issues unique digital verification tokens to validate active subscriptions.
  • Unity Runtime Engine: Evaluates default hardware properties (hardware configuration profiles, active operating system layers, and unique machine instances) to guarantee rendering parity and core system performance.

2. How We Use Your Information

We leverage incoming system signals and voluntarily declared telemetry data strictly for the following operational workflows:

  • To Facilitate Generative AI Tasks: Sending payload text inputs directly to core Gemini endpoints to fetch correct homework resolutions and reference summaries.
  • To Administer Purchase Events: Processing subscription privileges and verifying feature licenses using the built-in Google Play Billing components.
  • To Serve Contextual or Personalized Advertisements: Managing banner, interstitial, and rewarded ad inventory served via Google AdMob to help keep basic features free.
  • To Optimize System Stability: Processing crash dumps, exception states, and runtime errors across various Android hardware baselines.

3. Generative AI Data Safety Rules (Gemini API Integration)

Your academic requests and context questions are passed safely to Google's backend Gemini API endpoints to yield rapid textbook answers. We adhere strictly to safe deployment models:

  • We do not ingest your custom prompt inputs or chat logs into external matrices to train or adjust our baseline generative models.
  • Your historical logs remain entirely ephemeral or save directly to local app storage blocks unless an off-device cloud storage option is explicitly selected.
  • We explicitly advise that you do not insert sensitive metadata or personal credentials (including financial summaries, physical credentials, or government identifications) into the active prompt workspace.

4. Data Distribution and Shared Frameworks

Eleven App Studios does not sell, lease, or distribute private user data assets. Information paths interact solely with third-party infrastructure components mandatory for application operations:

  • Google (AdMob & Play Billing Services): Data pipelines run according to structural protections established inside the official Google Privacy and Terms documentation.
  • Unity Technologies: Operational telemetry complies fully with the operational mandates found in the Unity Game Player and App User Privacy Policy.

We reserve the right to pass operational logs forward if demanded by structural state mandates, formal court rules, or to shield our infrastructure from active safety threats.

5. Children's Privacy Mandates (COPPA Frameworks)

Atlas AI acts as a dedicated study tool. We apply careful protection baselines depending on age targeting parameters:

Under 13 Governance: If your target deployment configurations explicitly serve children under 13, we enforce adherence to the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). We do not collect verifiable private telemetry from children under 13 without structural parental indicators. Our AdMob modules are coded to request strictly non-personalized, child-safe inventory packages via active tag_for_child_directed_treatment flags.

If you are a parent or legal guardian and discover that a child under 13 has transmitted identifying records without validation, notify our response queue immediately so we can clear the database footprint.

6. Access Rights, Location Variances, and Deletion

Depending on geographical location parameters (including the European Economic Area under GDPR, or California under CCPA/CPRA frameworks), you have the right to request comprehensive account records extraction or permanent record purge sweeps.

  • You can modify or flush active advertising telemetry records instantly by navigating to your internal Android Device Settings system panel and resetting your personal Advertising ID.
  • To execute deep cloud record deletion routines for historical data patterns linked to your unique hardware configuration, please send a structured request to our support inbox.
